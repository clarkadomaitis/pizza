PIZZA OUTDOOR DINING

Pizza Places in Marine Park Are Participating in Indoor Dining, For the Time Being...

BROOKLYN - Marine Park is a neighborhood in the southeastern corner of Brooklyn. The neighborhood is home to seven community pizza places.

Indoor dining was made legal in NYC on September 30, after over 6 months of being shut down due to the coronavirus pandemic. The NYC restaurant industry has been devastated due to the closures. According to a report from the NYS Comptroller, by August 2020, employment in the restaurant industry was only “55 percent of its level in February 2020, before the pandemic hit.”

NYS’ regulations for indoor dining in the city to reopen include:

  • Maximum of 25% capacity
  • No bar service
  • Temperature checks required at the door
  • Masks must be worn at all times except for customers when seated
  • Restaurants close at midnight
  • “Should operate with enhanced air filtration” and ventilation

All seven pizza places in the neighborhood have decided to open tables for indoor dining. As of March, pizza places have only been able to open for delivery, take-out, and outdoor dining. The grab-and-go mentality of pizza places has allowed them to function during the pandemic. Lenny and John’s in early September was even considering not participating in indoor dining. “It's not something that we want to inflict on our customers, having them forward over all their personal information, having to take their temperatures. It's not appropriate for the type of business that we do.”

Marine Park is in one of the 11 zip codes that Mayor Bill de Blasio announced on October 4 that are seeing increasing COVID-19 infections. The current 14-day positivity rate is 2.22% in the 11234 zip code. If Marine Park's numbers rise over 3%, they may see a shutdown of indoor dining and outdoor dining.
